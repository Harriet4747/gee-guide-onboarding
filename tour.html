04Give it a job

Say it once. It holds the job.

Hand your worker a standing job in plain words — like briefing a sharp new hire. It keeps that job between the times you talk to it, and works it on its own.

Your worker's standing job
Set the limits in the same place. You decide what it does on its own and what waits for your yes — any send, spend, or change can hold for your approval.
05Connect its tools

Plug in your tools. Bring your model(s).

Your worker works inside the tools you already use — not in a silo you copy-paste into. And you choose the AI under the hood: bring your model(s), and toggle between them yourself or let Geenius toggle for you. Swap in a better one whenever it ships; your workers stay.

Email Calendar Drive & docs Messaging Your model(s)
You grant each connection on purpose, and pull it back in a click. A worker only touches what you've allowed, and sends from your Gee inbox unless you hand it an outside account.
06It remembers, and keeps going

It picks the work back up — on its own.

Your worker remembers across days and wakes itself on a schedule to move things forward, no prompt required. You watch it in the canvas, with a checkpoint whenever it matters.

Mon 4:58 pm
Sent the weekly update to 9 clients. Filed a copy in Drive.
Tue 9:02 am
No reply from Acme — sent a friendly nudge. You didn't have to remember.
Wed 8:40 am
Two invoices crossed 30 days — drafted the follow-ups, holding for your OK.
Every run shows what it cost. The model you picked is your cost dial — set a budget per worker, and it checks in before it runs past the cap.
07Stay in control

It's powerful because you set the rules.

Three dials keep a worker firmly yours. Set them when you create it; change them anytime.

Guardrails & approvals
Decide what runs on its own and what waits for your yes. Checkpoints on anything that sends, spends, or changes something.
Permissions & security
Grant each tool on purpose, revoke in a click. Workers send from your Gee inbox by default; outside accounts only when you hand them over.
Spend & cost
Pick the model that fits your budget. Track what each worker and workflow costs, and cap it so nothing surprises you.
